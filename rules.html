Cyber Clash rulebook

Game overview

Cyber Clash pits Hackers against Defenders. The Hackers aim to shut down the city's IT services, while the Defenders strive to protect these systems for as long as possible.

Required Materials

  • The game board
  • Zone tokens
  • Player pawns
  • Hacker cards
  • Defender cards
  • Zone cards
  • Chance cards

Setup

  • 1. Create a deck with the Hacker cards (use the illustrated back of the cards).
  • 2. Create a deck with the Defender cards (use the illustrated back of the cards).
  • 3. Create a deck with the Zone cards (use the illustrated back of the cards).
  • 4. Create a deck with the Chance cards (use the illustrated back of the cards).
  • 5. Place a green token on all buildings.
    • A building can have the following statuses:
      • Blue: Secured
      • Green: Operational
      • Yellow: Warning
      • Red: Shutdown
  • 6. Assign roles: Half of the players are Hackers, and the other half are Defenders. (An odd number of players is not allowed.)
  • Each player starts with 5 energy.

Game Start

  • 1. Each Hacker draws a Zone card and shares it with the other Hackers
    • These Zone cards determine the target buildings. All target buildings must simultaneously have a Red status for the Hackers to win.
  • Deal 3 Hacker cards or 3 Defender cards to each player, depending on their role.
  • All players begin at the Metro Station.

Gameplay

The youngest Hacker goes first.

  • Hacker's Turn:
    • Once per turn, draw a Hacker card.
    • Roll a die to determine movement.
    • Use energy to play cards.
  • Defender's Turn:
    • Once per turn, draw a Defender card.
    • Roll a die to determine movement.
    • Use energy to play cards

End of Game

  • The Hackers win if they manage to hack all target buildings before the end of 15 turns.
  • The Defenders win if they successfully protect the systems for 15 turns.

Cards

Cards Definition
Defender cards in blue and purple: used to secure buildings.
Hacker cards in red, yellow, orange, and black: used to hack buildings.
Chance cards in green: used to gain or lose energy points and additional cards.
Zone cards in blue: used by hackers to determine which buildings they will hack.

Glossary

  • Phishing : An online fraud technique that involves deceiving a victim into providing sensitive information (such as passwords or credit card numbers) by pretending to be a legitimate entity via counterfeit emails, messages, or websites.
  • SQL Injection : An attack where malicious code is inserted into an SQL query, exploiting a vulnerability in a web application to access or manipulate a database, often to steal information or damage data.
  • Ransomware : A type of malicious software that encrypts the user's files and demands a ransom for their decryption. It can spread through emails or vulnerabilities in systems.
  • DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) : An attack that overwhelms a server or network with a large number of requests from multiple sources, aiming to make a service or website inaccessible to legitimate users.
  • VPN (Virtual Private Network) and Encryption : A VPN is a service that creates a secure and private connection on the internet. Encryption is a process that makes data unreadable to anyone who does not have the decryption key, ensuring the confidentiality of transmitted information.
  • Identity Theft : The act of impersonating another person, typically to commit fraud, by using stolen personal information (such as social security numbers, bank cards, etc.).
  • Tor and Dark Web : Tor is a network that allows for anonymous browsing by hiding the user's IP address. The dark web is a part of the internet not indexed by traditional search engines, often associated with illegal activities and enhanced anonymity.
  • Personal Firewall : A security device that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic on a device (such as a computer or smartphone), to protect against intrusions and unauthorized access.
  • Trace Removal : The process by which a user or software deliberately deletes traces left on a device or network, such as browsing history, temporary files, or login logs, often to avoid being tracked.
  • Botnet : A network of infected devices controlled remotely by an attacker, often used to carry out DDoS attacks, send spam, or perform other malicious activities.
  • Trojan Horse (Trojan) : A type of malware disguised as a legitimate or useful program, but designed to perform malicious actions once installed on the target system, such as stealing data or allowing unauthorized remote access.
  • Network Analysis : The process of examining network traffic to identify anomalies, suspicious behaviors, or performance issues. It helps monitor security, optimize networks, and detect potential attacks.
  • Intrusion Detection System (IDS) : A system designed to monitor networks and systems to detect suspicious or malicious activities that may indicate an intrusion attempt. IDS alerts administrators so they can respond quickly.
  • IP Tracing : A technique that allows locating a device on a network by identifying its IP address. It helps determine the origin of a connection or online attack.
  • Log Analysis : The process of reviewing logs generated by systems, applications, and devices to spot anomalies, errors, or signs of malicious activities. Logs provide crucial information for understanding security incidents.
  • System Updates : The process of installing the latest versions of software, operating systems, and applications to fix vulnerabilities, improve performance, and add new features. Updates are essential to protect against security threats.
  • Firewall Activation : The action of enabling a firewall, a security device that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic to block unauthorized connections and protect systems from attacks.
  • Data Encryption : The process of transforming data into an unreadable format for anyone unauthorized, using an encryption algorithm. This ensures the confidentiality of information, even in the event of theft or interception.
  • System Backup:The creation of backup copies of systems, applications, and data to protect them from loss or corruption. Regular backups allow for the restoration of information in the event of system failure or attack.
  • Access Blocking : The act of restricting or preventing access to a system, network, or specific resource, often as part of security management to prevent unauthorized access or respond to an active threat.
  • Behavioral Analysis : A technique that involves monitoring and analyzing the behavior of users or systems to detect anomalies that could indicate malicious or suspicious activity. Behavioral analysis can be used to detect "zero-day" attacks or unconventional intrusions.
  • Password Reset : The process of changing a password, typically in the case of a compromise or suspicion of unauthorized access. This helps secure accounts and prevent illegal access.
  • System Quarantine:The act of isolating an infected system or device to prevent the spread of malware or other threats. This process may be temporary until the threat is eliminated and the system is cleaned.
